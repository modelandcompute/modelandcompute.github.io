Submit Your Paper

Workshop Summary

Current machine learning (ML) increasingly depends on scaling up data, model size, and compute resources to reach state-of-the-art performance. While this strategy has driven remarkable progress, it also raises critical challenges: longer runtimes, higher memory and energy demands, inefficient hardware utilization, and limited deployment scalability. The current scaling-centric approach often neglects the cumulative costs across the computational stack, threatening ML's sustainability and accessibility.

MOCOCO (MOdeling and COmputation CO-Design for Efficient Machine Learning) offers a venue to rethink ML through the joint design of modeling and computation. It promotes efficiency-driven co-design across model architecture, learning algorithms, software, and hardware. From general to domain-specific ML in science and healthcare, MOCOCO welcomes work that aligns efficiency objectives across all layers of the ML pipeline.

Activities. The workshop will include keynotes, a panel discussion, oral presentations, and poster sessions. We invite high-quality original papers of up to 4 pages. All accepted papers will be presented as posters, with a subset selected for oral presentations.

Attendance Estimate. We expect between 80 and 100 paper submissions and around 200 attendees.

Workshop Highlights

We highlight the contributions of this workshop in crystallizing problems and emerging scientific paradigms towards efficient ML:

  • ▪ Bridging Modeling and Computation for Efficiency: MOCOCO pioneers a co-design approach that unifies modeling, algorithm design, and system-level innovations. By moving beyond isolated solutions, it fosters integrated strategies that maximize ML efficiency across the stack, from architecture to hardware, marking a significant shift in how integrative efficient ML systems are developed and deployed.
  • ▪ Industry-Academia Collaboration at the Core: The workshop is intentionally structured to catalyze partnerships between academia and industry. With organizers and speakers from top research institutions and leading technology companies like Meta and Lambda, MOCOCO exemplifies a practical and research-driven collaboration framework aimed at real-world impact.
  • ▪ Diverse and Intergenerational Organizing Team: MOCOCO is distinct in its balanced involvement of both junior and senior researchers in the organization and speaker lineup. This design promotes mentorship, fresh perspectives, and continuity in advancing the field of efficient ML, encouraging broad community engagement.
  • ▪ Strong Industrial Sponsorship by Lambda: Backed by Lambda as a secured sponsor, MOCOCO not only ensures financial support for outstanding work but also includes industry demonstrations. This backing reinforces the workshop's relevance to commercial scalability and provides tangible incentives such as GPU credit awards to recognize impactful research.
  • ▪ Interdisciplinary Integration with NIH BRAIN Initiative Expert: Highlighting the intersection of ML and biomedical informatics, a dedicated session by Prof. Jim Zheng will present compute and data challenges from the NIH BRAIN Initiative. This elevates MOCOCO's scope to include critical applications in health and science, broadening its impact and attracting interdisciplinary participation.

Call for Papers

Submission Guidelines

We invite researchers and practitioners to submit their original work on modeling and computation co-design for efficient machine learning. Please refer to the Workshop Scope section for detailed topics of interest.

Paper Format: Papers should be formatted according to the NeurIPS 2025 style guidelines and should not exceed 4 pages (excluding references and appendices).

Submission Deadline: September 30, 2025, Anywhere on Earth (AoE)

Awards: Two outstanding papers and two outstanding student papers will be recognized by the program committee.

Submit Your Paper

Scope & Diversity

Paper Topics

We welcome papers with topics as follows:

1. Data-efficient modeling beyond auto-regressive modeling

(e.g., discrete diffusion models and latent thought vectors for language modeling)

2. Unified efficient modeling across multi-modalities, including text, vision, audio and genes

(e.g., unified tokenizer for vision and language)

3. Efficient model architecture beyond Transformers

(e.g., state-space models, convolution-based models, hybrid models)

4. Hardware-aware ML algorithms beyond giant matrix multiplication for efficient training and inference

(e.g., sparsity-driven computing, low-rank algorithms, tensor decomposition)

5. Benchmarks to evaluate accuracy-efficiency trade-offs of ML systems

(e.g., benchmark efficiency in LLM reasoning, long-context understanding)

6. Co-design of modeling, algorithm, system and hardware for efficient ML

(e.g., sparse ML systems with customized hardware such as FPGA)

7. Efficient ML for driving accelerated scientific discovery

(e.g., accelerated material design, gene pathway discovery)
